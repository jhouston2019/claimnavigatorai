Common Questions

What documents should I upload?

Upload any documents you have: denial letters, insurance estimates, engineer reports, contractor estimates, policy documents, or claim correspondence. The system will analyze whatever you provide and identify what's missing.

Can Claim Command explain denial letters?

Yes. The system extracts the insurer's stated reasoning from denial letters and correspondence, identifies the dispute category (causation, scope, coverage, or documentation), and explains what the insurer is actually arguing in plain English.

Can it analyze estimates and engineer reports?

Yes. When you upload insurance estimates and contractor estimates, the system performs line-by-line comparison to identify pricing gaps and missing scope items. When engineer reports are uploaded, it extracts the engineer's conclusions and identifies potential dispute points.

What do I see before I pay?

Before payment, you'll see an instant insight preview that includes: key insurer statements extracted from your documents, likely dispute category, potential documentation gaps, and estimate issues (if estimates were uploaded). This preview proves the system understands your specific claim before you commit to the full report.

What if my claim was already denied?

Many denials stem from documentation gaps, not true coverage issues. The system analyzes the denial reasoning, identifies what proof is missing, and provides structured appeal templates and documentation checklists to reopen the claim.

Can contractors or adjusters use this report?

Yes. The full report is structured for professional use. Contractors can use it to understand the dispute faster and prepare accurate estimates. Public adjusters and attorneys can use it as a foundation for their work. The report includes share and download options.

Is this legal representation?

No. Claim Command Pro provides claim analysis and documentation tools. It does not provide legal advice or act as a public adjuster. The system helps you understand your claim position and structure your documentation.
