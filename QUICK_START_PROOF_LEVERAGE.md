# 🚀 Quick Start: Proof & Leverage Layer

**5-Minute Guide to Using the New Features**

---

## 🎯 What's New?

The Claim Command Center now tells you **exactly what proof you need** and **when to escalate**. No more guessing!

---

## 📍 Where to Find New Features

### 1. **Financial Summary (Top of Page)**
**Look for:** "Next Required Action" section below the underpayment amount

**What it shows:**
- 🔴 Missing documentation (critical)
- 🟡 Code triggers not documented
- 🟡 High-value items without proof

**What to do:** Gather the listed documentation before proceeding to supplement

---

### 2. **Step 8: Estimate Comparison**
**Look for:** "What You Must Prove" section after running comparison

**What it shows:** For each disputed line item:
- ✓ Damage documentation required
- ✓ Causation explanation required
- ✓ Pricing support required
- ✓ Code citation required

**What to do:** Review the list and start gathering proof for high-value items first

---

### 3. **Step 9: Code & Coverage Analysis**
**Look for:** "View Coverage Triggers & Required Proof" button

**What it shows:** Checklist of required documentation:
- ☐ Building department code citation (for ordinance coverage)
- ☐ Photo evidence of mismatch (for matching endorsement)
- ☐ Other policy-specific requirements

**What to do:** Check boxes as you gather each piece of proof

---

### 4. **Step 10: Supplement Generation**
**Look for:** Automatic documentation section in generated supplement

**What it shows:**
- If proof is missing: "Additional documentation forthcoming" + list of gaps
- If proof is complete: "Supporting Documentation Attached" + standard list

**What to do:** If gaps are listed, gather that proof before sending supplement

---

### 5. **Step 15: Escalation**
**Look for:** "Evaluate Escalation Status" button

**What it shows:**
- Escalation level (0-3)
- Recommendation (supervisor/DOI/appraisal)
- Days since supplement
- Days since last response

**What to do:** Click "Generate Template" to create the appropriate escalation letter

---

## 🎬 Quick Workflow

### First Time Through
1. **Run estimate comparison** (Step 8)
2. **Review "What You Must Prove"** section
3. **Gather documentation** for high-value items
4. **Check off proof requirements** in Step 9
5. **Generate supplement** (Step 10)
6. **Wait for carrier response**
7. **If no response after 10+ days:** Evaluate escalation (Step 15)

### When Carrier Doesn't Respond
1. Go to **Step 15**
2. Click **"Evaluate Escalation Status"**
3. Review recommendation:
   - **Level 1 (10+ days):** Supervisor escalation letter
   - **Level 2 (20+ days):** DOI complaint
   - **Level 3 (30+ days):** Appraisal demand
4. Click **"Generate Template"**
5. **Copy or download** the template
6. **Customize** with your specific details
7. **Send** to carrier/DOI

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Tip 1: Start with High-Value Items
Focus your proof-gathering efforts on line items with the biggest dollar impact first.

### Tip 2: Use the Checkboxes
As you gather proof in Step 9, check the boxes. This updates the system and ensures your supplement is accurate.

### Tip 3: Don't Wait Too Long
If 10 days pass with no response, start the escalation process. The system will tell you exactly what to do.

### Tip 4: Keep Documentation Organized
The system tells you what you need. Keep all proof organized by line item for easy reference.

### Tip 5: Use the Templates
The escalation templates are professionally written and include all necessary legal language. Just customize the bracketed fields.

## 🔍 Understanding Escalation Levels

### Level 0: No Escalation
- Claim is progressing normally
- Continue with standard process

### Level 1: Supervisor Escalation
- **Trigger:** Supplement submitted > 10 days ago
- **Action:** Request supervisor review
- **Template:** Supervisor escalation letter

### Level 2: DOI Complaint
- **Trigger:** Supplement submitted > 20 days, no response > 10 days
- **Action:** File complaint with Department of Insurance
- **Template:** DOI complaint form

### Level 3: Appraisal Clause
- **Trigger:** Supplement submitted > 30 days, no response > 15 days
- **Action:** Invoke appraisal clause in policy
- **Template:** Formal appraisal demand letter

## ❓ Common Questions

### Q: What if I don't have all the proof yet?
**A:** That's okay! The system will note "Additional documentation forthcoming" in your supplement. Gather the proof and submit it separately.

### Q: Can I customize the escalation templates?
**A:** Yes! The templates include bracketed fields like `[Your Name]` and `[Date]` that you should customize. The rest is professionally written and ready to use.

### Q: What if the carrier responds after I escalate?
**A:** Great! Document the response in the claim journal. The escalation level will automatically adjust based on the new activity.

### Q: Do I have to wait for escalation?
**A:** No, but it's recommended. The system uses industry-standard timelines. Escalating too early can hurt your negotiating position.

### Q: Can I mark proof as complete even if I haven't uploaded it?
**A:** Yes. The checkboxes track whether you've gathered the proof, not whether it's uploaded. Upload separately as needed.
