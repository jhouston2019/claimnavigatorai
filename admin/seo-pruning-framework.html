SEO Pruning Framework

Decision support tool for content consolidation and optimization

⚠️ Important: Guidance Only

This framework provides decision criteria for content consolidation. DO NOT delete pages without careful analysis. Always implement 301 redirects when merging content. This is internal documentation for strategic planning only.

Merge Criteria

Pages meeting multiple criteria below may be candidates for consolidation into stronger, more comprehensive content.

📊 Low Traffic Performance
Pages with less than 20 impressions in the last 90 days and no meaningful click-through activity.
Example: Page has 8 impressions, 0 clicks in 90 days
🔍 Poor Ranking Position
Pages ranking beyond position 50 for all tracked keywords with no upward trend over 6 months.
Example: Best ranking is position 67, no movement in 6 months
⚔️ Keyword Cannibalization
Multiple pages targeting the same primary keyword, splitting authority and confusing search engines.
Example: 3 pages all targeting "insurance claim help Texas"
🔄 Overlapping Topic Clusters
Pages covering nearly identical topics without sufficient differentiation or unique value.
Example: "How to file claim" and "Filing insurance claim" pages
📏 Thin Content
Pages with less than 500 words, minimal unique value, or outdated information that could be consolidated.
Example: 300-word page with generic information
🔗 Weak Backlink Profile
Pages with zero external backlinks and minimal internal link equity that could benefit from consolidation.
Example: No backlinks, only 2 internal links from footer
Content Consolidation Checklist
Identify merge target: Choose the stronger page with better rankings, more backlinks, or higher authority to serve as the consolidation destination.
Extract valuable content: Review the weaker page and identify any unique information, data, examples, or insights worth preserving.
Merge into stronger URL: Integrate the valuable content from the weak page into the target page, maintaining logical flow and structure.
Implement 301 redirect: Set up a permanent redirect from the old URL to the consolidated page to preserve any existing link equity.
Update internal links: Find and update all internal links pointing to the old URL to point directly to the consolidated page.
Update sitemap: Remove the old URL from sitemap.xml and ensure the consolidated page is included and properly prioritized.
Monitor performance: Track rankings, traffic, and indexing status for 30-60 days after consolidation to ensure no negative impact.
Request re-indexing: Submit the consolidated page to Google Search Console for re-crawling and indexing.
Best Practice: Never consolidate more than 3-5 pages per week. Monitor the impact of each consolidation before proceeding with additional merges.
