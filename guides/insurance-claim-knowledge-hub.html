Introduction

This Knowledge Hub is the topical root for Claim Command Pro's authority content. Whether you're a homeowner facing an underpaid claim, a commercial property owner navigating business interruption, or a policyholder weighing appraisal—this hub organizes everything you need.

The content is structured around five pillar guides (comprehensive playbooks) and seven topic clusters (specific problem areas). Each cluster links to targeted resource pages, and we provide interactive tools, research studies, and document templates to support your claim. Pillar guides give you the strategic framework; cluster pages address discrete questions like "How do I invoke appraisal?" or "What do I do if State Farm lowballed me?"

How to navigate: Use the table of contents above to jump to any section. If you're early in your claim, start with the Property Damage Documentation Blueprint and the Claim Documentation Checklist Generator. If you've already received an offer that's too low, begin with the Complete Negotiation Guide and the Settlement Increase Calculator. The clusters are organized by theme—pick the one that matches your situation.

Who this is for: Property owners with active or recent claims who want to understand their options, document effectively, negotiate strategically, and escalate when necessary—without relying on guesswork or costly intermediaries. We focus on documentation-first strategies that give you leverage at every stage. Our research shows that policyholders who document systematically and negotiate with evidence often recover 20–50% more than those who accept initial offers without challenge.

How to Use This Knowledge Base

Navigation tips: Start with the pillar guides if you're new to claim negotiation. They provide the strategic framework without assuming prior knowledge. Use the topic clusters when you have a specific problem—for example, if your offer is too low, go to Cluster A; if you need to submit a supplement, go to Cluster B. Carrier- and state-specific pages (Clusters F and G) are useful for context on deadlines, regulatory requirements, and common tactics by insurer or jurisdiction. Don't skip the Research section: the statistics and tactics reports help you understand what you're up against and set realistic expectations.

Recommended reading paths: For underpaid claims, read the Complete Negotiation Guide, then How Insurance Companies Calculate Settlements, then the supplement and estimate dispute pages in Cluster B. For appraisal, read the Appraisal Strategy Guide first, then Invoke Appraisal and the insurance appraisal process. For documentation, the Property Damage Documentation Blueprint is the starting point—combine it with the Claim Documentation Checklist Generator for a tailored checklist. If you're commercial, start with the Commercial Property Insurance Claim page and Business Interruption Claim Help before diving into the pillar guides.

When to use tools vs guides: Use the Settlement Increase Calculator when you have both a carrier estimate and a contractor estimate—it quantifies the gap and suggests a reasonable recovery range. Use the Depreciation Recovery Calculator when you have an RCV policy and know your depreciation withhold; it clarifies how much the carrier should release upon proof of repair. Use the Claim Documentation Checklist Generator early in the claim to ensure you capture everything by damage type and property type. Guides explain the why and how; tools help you apply the numbers. Both reinforce each other.

Document templates: The supplement, demand letter, and appraisal invocation templates are not generic fill-in-the-blank forms—they're structured guides that show you what to include and how to phrase it. Review the associated cluster pages (How to Write Insurance Supplement, Demand Letter to Insurance Company, Invoke Appraisal) for full context before drafting.
