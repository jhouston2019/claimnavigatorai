Overview

Response timelines govern how quickly insurers must answer inquiries and issue coverage decisions. Most states require carriers to provide written decisions within set periods after receiving proofs of loss or completing investigations. Clear, timely responses reduce uncertainty and allow policyholders to plan repairs or pursue dispute options.

Responses should cite policy provisions, facts, and the basis for any payment or denial. Partial denials require specificity—what is covered, what is not, and why. Silence or vague statements can breach fair claims standards and should be challenged in writing.

Policyholders should track when documents are submitted and when responses are due. If deadlines pass, request status and remind carriers of regulatory timelines. Escalate if repeated delays occur without explanation.

Thorough, dated records of submissions and carrier replies help demonstrate compliance and spotlight unreasonable delays.

Key Rules & Requirements

  • Carriers must respond to proofs of loss and inquiries within regulated timeframes.
  • Decisions must be in writing, citing policy provisions and factual bases.
  • Partial denials should separate covered vs. non-covered items clearly.
  • Status updates are required when investigations remain open for a reasonable time.
  • Unreasonable delay without explanation can violate fair claims standards.

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Submit required documents and note the date they are delivered.
  2. Calendar expected response deadlines per state standards or policy.
  3. If no response arrives, send a written status request referencing the due date.
  4. Request detailed explanations and supporting estimates for any adverse decision.
  5. Escalate to supervisors or DOI if delays or vague responses persist.

Required Documentation

  • Proof of loss and supporting submissions with dates sent.
  • Carrier responses and decision letters with dates received.
  • Communication log showing follow-ups and escalations.
  • Estimates and reports supporting the carrier’s position.

Common Insurer Tactics

  • Providing verbal updates without written confirmation.
  • Issuing vague denials without policy citations or factual support.
  • Resetting timelines after each new submission without justification.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Missed response deadlines after proofs of loss are submitted.
  • Status updates that repeat “under review” without substance.
  • Partial denials lacking explanation of excluded items.

Best Practices for Policyholders

  • Keep a calendar of submissions and expected response dates.
  • Request written decisions with policy citations; challenge vague responses.
  • Escalate timely when silence or inadequate responses persist.
