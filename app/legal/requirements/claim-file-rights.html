Overview

Access to the claim file allows policyholders to understand how decisions were made. While rules vary by state, many jurisdictions require carriers to provide non-privileged portions of the claim file upon request, including estimates, photos, reports, and communications that support coverage positions. Privileged materials (legal advice, work product) are typically excluded.

Timely access helps challenge errors, correct misunderstandings, and prepare supplements. Carriers should identify what is withheld and why. If the file is partially provided, request an index of withheld materials.

Requests should be specific: estimates, adjuster notes, photos, engineer reports, vendor invoices, and payment histories. Some states allow fees for copies; electronic delivery is often faster and reduces cost.

Maintaining your own complete record—including what was sent and received—strengthens any future dispute and helps align understanding of the loss and payments made.

Key Rules & Requirements

  • Policyholders may request non-privileged claim file materials that support decisions.
  • Carriers must identify what is provided and, where required, what is withheld as privileged.
  • Estimates, reports, photos, and payment logs are generally discoverable parts of the file.
  • Some states permit reasonable copy fees; electronic delivery is preferred to reduce cost.
  • Update requests if new inspections or reports are generated.

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Submit a written request for the non-privileged claim file, listing specific categories (estimates, photos, reports, communications, payment ledger).
  2. Ask the carrier to identify any withheld items and the basis for withholding.
  3. Review the file for accuracy; note discrepancies in scope, pricing, or factual assumptions.
  4. Provide clarifications or supplemental evidence to correct errors in the file.
  5. Request updates when new inspections, reports, or payments occur.

Required Documentation

  • Written request for claim file materials and response from the carrier.
  • Carrier-provided estimates, reports, photos, and payment history.
  • Your communication log to reconcile dates and actions.
  • Supplemental documents you submit to correct or expand the record.

Common Insurer Tactics

  • Delaying file production or providing incomplete sets without explanation.
  • Withholding vendor reports while relying on their conclusions.
  • Providing summaries instead of underlying estimates and photos.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • References to reports or photos that were not shared.
  • Payment decisions that cite estimates you have never seen.
  • Large gaps in the timeline compared to your communication log.

Best Practices for Policyholders

  • Request the claim file early and again after major milestones.
  • Keep your own organized repository with indexing to spot gaps quickly.
  • Challenge inaccuracies promptly with supporting evidence.
