Why deadlines matter

Deadlines control leverage, determine penalties, and can reset or shorten when evidence is late. Your policy clocks and your state’s statutory clocks often differ, and carriers sometimes set aggressive dates that are negotiable. Staying ahead keeps the claim compliant and defensible.

Carrier-set deadlines cover their response and action windows. State statutory deadlines govern acknowledgments, investigations, and payment timing. Knowing both lets you push back on unreasonable demands and escalate when timelines slip.

State-level deadlines overview

States layer their own clocks on top of your policy. They dictate when carriers must acknowledge, investigate, decide, and pay. Knowing the local rules keeps pressure on the carrier and prevents avoidable delays.

  • 10–15 day acknowledgment standards
  • 30-day acceptance or denial timelines
  • Payment windows that range from 10–20 days
  • Reasonableness standards for document requests

Common traps to avoid

Prevent avoidable delays and keep every clock intact.

Missed Proof of Loss deadline

Track the policy and statutory windows so extensions or tolling requests go out before the deadline passes.

Submitting evidence too close to deadlines

Last-minute uploads can shorten carrier response clocks; stagger submissions to keep timelines favorable.

Delays caused by incomplete submission

Incomplete packages let carriers pause the clock; use checklists to meet documentation standards on the first pass.

Carrier delay tactics

Silence, repeated document requests, or rotating adjusters can stall progress—log and challenge unreasonable resets.

Letting silence extend timelines

Follow up with dated notices when responses are overdue so interest and penalties accrue.

Accepting unreasonable deadlines

Push back on aggressive dates that conflict with policy or statutory rights and propose realistic timelines.

What To Do Right Now
