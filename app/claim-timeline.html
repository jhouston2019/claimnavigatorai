Overview

This guide outlines the correct chronological order of a property insurance claim, including required carrier actions, deadlines, documentation steps, and escalation triggers.

Claim Sequence

Follow these events in order. Stay proactive with documentation and deadlines.

01

First Notice of Loss (FNOL)

Formally notify the carrier of the loss with date, cause, affected areas, and preferred contact. This starts statutory response clocks.

02

Acknowledgment of Claim

The carrier must confirm receipt and assign an adjuster within the state’s acknowledgment window. Capture the adjuster’s contact and claim number.

03

Inspection Scheduling

An inspection date is set. Confirm scope (interior, roof, contents) and who will attend. Provide access notes and safety concerns in advance.

04

Inspection Completed

Carrier inspects the loss site. Capture who attended, what was photographed, and any verbal commitments. Note missing areas for follow-up.

Carrier’s Initial Estimate

Carrier issues a written estimate or explanation. Compare scope, pricing, and code items against your contractor bids and photos.

Dispute or Reinspection Phase

If scope or pricing is off, request a reinspection and include side-by-side comparisons. Keep written records of requests and carrier responses.

Common Carrier Delays

Missing documentation requests

Respond with a dated list of what you already sent and resubmit in one organized packet.

Delayed acknowledgment

Escalate after the statutory window; log every outreach with timestamps.

Slow estimate release

Request a delivery date in writing and cite state timelines if applicable.

No response > 14 days

Send a follow-up letter, copy a supervisor, and set a firm reply deadline.

Reinspection stall tactics

Offer two time windows and document refusals; send photos and scope notes meanwhile.

Adjuster changes

Re-send a concise case brief with all prior commitments to avoid resets.

What to Do if Carrier Stops Responding

  • Send a dated follow-up citing prior attempts and the expected response window.
  • Escalate to a supervisor and request a call time; keep all notes in your journal.
  • Reference applicable state claim-handling deadlines in your message.
  • Prepare a complaint draft if silence continues; attach your communication log.

Required Documentation Timing

Photos

Capture initial loss photos immediately and repeat after mitigation or repairs.

Receipts

Collect receipts as incurred; upload weekly to avoid missing reimbursements.

Proof of Loss

Prepare once estimates stabilize; submit before the policy or state deadline.

Supplemental docs

Send new estimates or invoices within 3–5 days of receipt and request written confirmation.

Legal Time Limits

Statutory deadlines

State laws set acknowledgment and decision timelines; track each date in your journal.

Policy deadlines

Proof of Loss, suit limitation, and supplemental filing windows are defined in the policy.

Bad faith triggers

Unreasonable delays or underpayment after evidence is provided may support a bad faith claim.
