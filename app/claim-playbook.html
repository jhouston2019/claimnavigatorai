The Complete Insurance Claim Management Playbook

Your comprehensive guide to maximizing every insurance claim. You carry the burden of proof—the insurer pays what you can prove. This Playbook shows you exactly how to secure, document, file, respond, negotiate, and close your claim — with AI support at every step.

How to use: Work section by section. Each area includes examples, decision trees, and an If → Then → Tool rail at the end so you always know the next best move and the right Claim Navigator tool to click.

1. Pre-Claim Preparation

Policy Documentation System

Action Required: Create a comprehensive binder: dec pages, riders/endorsements, premium proof (3y), all correspondence, prior claims, renewals, coverage changes.

Example: John's RCV endorsement on hand avoided an ACV-only opening offer and shaved 3 days off processing.

Digital Backup

  • Cloud folders: Year → Policy Type → Document Type
  • Password-protect sensitive docs; share with trusted contact
  • Quarterly updates

Request certified copy citing state timeframe

Run coverage decoder and flag endorsements
Home Inventory Documentation

Room-by-room list with model/serials, purchase date, cost & current replacement; photos (multi-angle), receipts, video walkthrough narration.

Case: Sarah's theft claim recovered $47k vs $28k initial due to receipts + appraisals + annual video.

If → Then → Tool

No receipts for older items
Use alt proofs: bank/CC statements, Amazon history, photos, social posts, warranty regs
High-value items (jewelry/art)
Obtain/refresh appraisals; check sub-limits and schedule
Pre-Loss Property Documentation

Monthly exterior photos (all sides/roofline), systems (HVAC/plumbing/electrical), flooring and finishes; date-stamp. Counters "pre-existing wear."

If → Then → Tool

Carrier claims pre-existing
Submit prior-condition photos and maintenance logs

2. Immediate Post-Loss Actions

Hour 1–2: Safety & Mitigation

Fire

  • Evacuate; wait for clearance
  • Board-up if needed; save invoices
  • Start safe-distance photos

Water

  • Shut off source; video active leak
  • Emergency extraction; dry & dehumidify
  • Move undamaged items
Critical: Policies require reasonable mitigation. Don't wait for adjuster permission—document and keep receipts.

If → Then → Tool

Home is uninhabitable
Request ALE advance and lodging authorization
Vendor requires insurer pre-approval
Proceed with emergency-only scope; notify carrier in writing with photos
Hour 2–6: Documentation

Wide → medium → close photos; serials; water/smoke lines; undamaged comparisons; written log of time/cause/weather/witnesses/actions.

If → Then → Tool

Lost critical receipts
Pull bank/CC PDFs; request store duplicates; annotate inventory
Hour 6–24: Initial Claim Contact

Prepare policy #, time/date, cause, report numbers, temp contact, emergency expenses. Use factual script.

If → Then → Tool

No acknowledgment in state timeframe
Send Delay Notice citing statute; request written status

Phase 2 – File the Claim Properly (Complete & Compliant)

1) Build Your Initial Submission Packet
  1. Cover Letter: Basic facts, claim #, date of loss, short damage summary, attachments list.
  2. Damage Inventory Sheet: Room-by-room contents list (qty, age, condition, price, source).
  3. Proof of Loss (POL): Policy #, cause, claimed amount, signature.
  4. Evidence Bundle: Photos/videos, receipts, mitigation invoices, contractor estimates.
Pro Tip: Use consistent filenames (e.g., Kitchen_Cabinets_Receipts.pdf) and reference them inside your letter for clarity.
2) Policy Mapping (Coverage A–D)

Map each claimed item to a coverage bucket:

  • A – Dwelling: Structure, finishes
  • B – Other Structures: Detached garage, shed, fence
  • C – Personal Property: Contents
  • D – Loss of Use: ALE/temporary housing
3) Deductibles, Limits, Sub-limits

Calculate deductible impact; check special sub-limits (jewelry, cash, business property at home, mold limits).

4) Submit & Confirm

Submit via the carrier portal or email. Immediately send a confirmation email listing all attachments and requesting a written acknowledgment + inspection date.

If → Then → Tool (Phase 2)

Carrier requests "more info" without specificity
Ask for a precise list of missing docs
Carrier rejects your inventory format
Regenerate in their columns, keep your master

Phase 3 – Adjuster Interaction (Inspection + Communications)

1) Pre-Inspection Prep
  • Create a Scope Walk List by room with must-see items.
  • Lay out receipts, estimates, and mitigation invoices in one folder.
  • Decide who speaks; avoid speculative opinions; stick to facts.
2) During Inspection

Walk room by room. Show hidden/less obvious damage (behind cabinets, under flooring). Photograph the adjuster measuring key areas (context for later).

Recorded statements: If requested on the spot, you can defer until prepared. Use the Recorded Statement Guide to plan answers.
3) Post-Inspection Confirmation

Send an email summarizing what was examined, any areas deferred, and next steps with dates.

If → Then → Tool (Phase 3)

Adjuster rushes & skips rooms
Politely request full walk-through; document refusal
Adjuster makes verbal promise
Confirm by email within 24h

Phase 4 – Documentation Mastery

1) Folder Structure & Naming

Mirror this structure digitally (and optionally physically): Policy / Photos / Receipts / Estimates / Insurer Letters / Diary / AI Docs.

2) Evidence Hygiene
  • Use OCR PDFs
  • Embed dates in filenames
  • Store originals + watermarked share copies
  • Hash or checksum large files (optional)
3) Estimates & Expert Reports

Obtain at least one independent estimate; for complex losses consider specialists (industrial hygienist, structural engineer).

If → Then → Tool (Phase 4)

Carrier says "insufficient documentation"
Request a specific itemized list of what is missing
Carrier rejects contractor estimate
Submit competing estimate + code citations

Phase 5 – Decode Insurer Letters

1) Letter Triage
  • Acknowledgment/Next Steps
  • Request for Info (RFI)
  • Coverage Position (accept/partial/deny)
  • Payment Explanation (ACV/RCV/depreciation)
2) Common Tactics
  • "Wear & tear" catch-all
  • "Pre-existing" assertions
  • Low scope pricing
  • Excessive depreciation
  • Repeated generic RFIs
  • Delay loops

If → Then → Tool (Phase 5)

Partial denial citing exclusion
Request precise clause + facts + photos relied upon
Payment shows heavy depreciation
Challenge depreciation schedule with market lifespan sources

Phase 6 – Strategic Responses

1) Match Response to Letter Type
  • Delay → Notice of Delay + State Rule
  • RFI (vague) → Specificity Demand
  • Partial Denial → Appeal (facts + clauses + evidence)
  • Lowball Offer → Line-item Rebuttal + comparables
2) Cite State Rules

Use State Rule Lookup + AI Agent to insert acknowledgment, investigation, payment, and communication timeframe rules into your letters.

If → Then → Tool (Phase 6)

Carrier stops responding
Send escalation notice with statutory timelines + regulator CC
Carrier cites "wear & tear" vaguely
Request engineering basis + photos + tests relied upon

Phase 7 – Escalation & Negotiation

1) Escalation Options
  • Appraisal (scope/pricing disputes)
  • Regulator Complaint (unfair claims practices)
  • Public Adjuster (representation/negotiation)
  • Attorney (coverage/denial/bad-faith)
State callout: Appraisal clauses vary by policy and state practice. Verify if mandated, optional, or waived by conduct.
2) Negotiation Framework
  • Anchor with your full documented scope.
  • Trade issues, not positions: pricing lines, code upgrades, timelines.
  • Always add new proof when moving on price.

If → Then → Tool (Phase 7)

Carrier refuses appraisal
Cite policy language + seek regulator guidance or counsel
Negotiations stall
Escalate with timeline leverage + documentation packet

Phase 8 – Offer & Settlement Review

1) Line-by-Line Comparison

Compare carrier scope/estimate to your master. Flag missing line items, underpriced trades, and code upgrades.

2) ACV vs RCV & Depreciation

Confirm whether payment is actual cash value (ACV) or replacement cost value (RCV) and the holdback terms. Challenge unreasonable depreciation.

3) Settlement Letter & Reservations

When accepting partial payment, preserve rights to pursue supplements or appeals.

If → Then → Tool (Phase 8)

Offer omits code upgrades
Submit code citations + inspector notes
Carrier delays payment after agreement
Send payment deadline reminder with state rule cite

Phase 9 – Supplements & Appeals

1) When to Supplement
  • Hidden damage discovered during repairs
  • New expert findings
  • Pricing changes or code mandates
2) Formal Appeal Package
  1. Appeal Letter (issues + relief requested)
  2. Policy citations & state rules
  3. Evidence index + exhibits
  4. Timeline of correspondence

If → Then → Tool (Phase 9)

Appeal window is short
Send intent-to-appeal preserving rights, then follow with full package
Appeal ignored
Escalate to regulator/counsel with record of delay

Phase 10 – Closeout & Prevention

1) Final Accounting
  • Confirm all payments (ACV + holdback) and categories (A–D).
  • Match checks to estimate lines; reconcile differences.
  • Retain claim archive for 7–10 years (and digital backup).
2) Releases & Reservations

Avoid overbroad releases that waive supplements/unknowns unless you're truly done and paid.

3) Prevention & Readiness
  • Annual photo/video scan of home
  • Receipt vaulting for big items
  • Policy review before renewal
  • Inventory update (room-by-room)

C) Glossary (Plain English)
  • ACV: Actual Cash Value (RCV minus depreciation).
  • RCV: Replacement Cost Value (full replacement price).
  • Depreciation: Reduction for age/condition; often recoverable when repairs completed (RCV policies).
  • Appraisal: Policy process to resolve scope/pricing disputes via neutral appraisers and umpire.
  • Duties After Loss: Your obligations post-loss (mitigation, documentation, cooperation, etc.).
D) State-Specific Issues (Use Lookup)

Deadlines and insurer duties (acknowledgment, investigation, payment timing, claim communications, unfair practices) vary by state and policy type. Use the State Rule Lookup with the AI Response Agent to insert the correct citations into your letters.

  • Examples to watch: time to acknowledge claim; time to decide/deny; prompt payment rules; appraisal handling; interest/penalties for late payment; required disclosures.
  • Specialty lines: Condo master vs unit policy, business interruption documentation, code upgrade endorsements, anti-concurrent causation wording.
