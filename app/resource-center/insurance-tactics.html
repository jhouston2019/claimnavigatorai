‚è∞ Delay Tactics
Insurance companies often use delay tactics to wear down claimants and pressure them into accepting lower settlements. Here's how to recognize and counter these tactics.
1
"We're Still Investigating"
Claims adjusters may claim they need more time to investigate, even for straightforward cases.
Your Response: "I understand you need time to investigate. Can you provide a specific timeline for completion? I'll follow up on [specific date] for an update."
2
"Waiting for Documentation"
They may claim they're waiting for documents you've already provided or request unnecessary information.
Your Response: "I provided that documentation on [date]. Let me resend it and confirm receipt. What other information do you need specifically?"
3
"Manager Review Required"
Claims may be passed between multiple people, creating unnecessary delays.
Your Response: "I'd like to speak directly with the manager handling this review. Can you provide their contact information and a timeline for their decision?"

üí∞ Lowball Offer Tactics
Insurance companies often make initial offers that are significantly below the actual value of your claim, hoping you'll accept without question.
1
"This is Our Standard Rate"
They may claim their offer is based on standard rates, even when it's below market value.
Your Response: "I've researched current market rates in this area. Your offer is significantly below what other contractors are charging. Here's my documentation."
2
"Depreciation Deductions"
They may apply excessive depreciation to reduce the settlement amount.
Your Response: "I'd like to see the depreciation schedule you're using. Can you provide documentation of how you calculated these deductions?"
3
"Policy Limits"
They may claim your policy has lower limits than it actually does.
Your Response: "I'd like a copy of my complete policy and a breakdown of coverage limits. I want to verify this information."

üìã Documentation Tactics
Insurance companies may use documentation requirements as a way to delay or deny claims. Learn to recognize these tactics and respond effectively.
1
"We Need More Proof"
They may request excessive documentation or proof that's difficult to obtain.
Your Response: "I've provided all reasonable documentation. What specific additional proof do you need, and why is it necessary for this claim?"
2
"Original Receipts Only"
They may insist on original receipts for items that may have been lost in the incident.
Your Response: "The original receipts were lost in the incident. I can provide bank statements, credit card records, and other documentation of purchase."
3
"Expert Assessment Required"
They may require expensive expert assessments for straightforward damage.
Your Response: "I can provide multiple contractor estimates. If you require an expert assessment, will the insurance company cover the cost?"

üò∞ Pressure Tactics
Insurance companies may use psychological pressure to get you to accept lower settlements or make hasty decisions.
1
"Limited Time Offer"
They may claim the offer is only valid for a short time to pressure you into accepting.
Your Response: "I need time to review this offer properly. I'll respond within a reasonable timeframe, but I won't be pressured into a hasty decision."
2
"This is Our Final Offer"
They may claim the offer is final when it's actually negotiable.
Your Response: "I understand this is your current offer. I'll review it and provide my response. If this is truly final, please provide that in writing."
3
"You're Being Unreasonable"
They may try to make you feel unreasonable for questioning their offer.
Your Response: "I'm simply seeking fair compensation based on the actual damages. I have documentation to support my position."
