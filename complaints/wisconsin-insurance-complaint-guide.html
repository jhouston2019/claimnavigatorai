Wisconsin Insurance Complaint Guide: File with OCI

Step-by-step guide to filing complaints with the Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance when your claim is denied, delayed, or underpaid.

Michael Chen • Insurance Claim Documentation Expert

Last reviewed: February 28, 2026

⚠️ Wisconsin policyholders who file OCI complaints with thorough documentation often see settlement increases of $10,000-$40,000. The state's established bad faith framework provides strong consumer protection.

When to File an OCI Complaint

File a complaint with the Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance when your insurance company engages in practices that violate Wisconsin Statutes Chapter 628 or your policy terms. Common triggers include:

  • Claim denial without reasonable investigation — Carrier denies without inspecting damage, reviewing documentation, or providing adequate explanation with policy citations
  • Unreasonable delay in processing — Carrier fails to acknowledge claim within 5 working days, delays investigation beyond reasonable timeframes, or fails to pay within 30 days of reaching agreement
  • Lowball settlement offers — Offer is 35-55% below documented repair costs with no reasonable justification, supporting evidence, or explanation of methodology
  • Refusal to negotiate in good faith — Carrier ignores demands, won't respond to documentation, makes take-it-or-leave-it offers, or refuses to explain valuation
  • Misrepresentation of policy terms — Carrier claims coverage doesn't exist when policy language clearly provides it, misquotes exclusions, or misrepresents policy limits
  • Bad faith tactics — Intimidation, threats to cancel policy, coercive settlement pressure, unreasonable documentation demands, or failure to explain rights

Wisconsin Bad Faith Law: What Qualifies

Wisconsin has well-established bad faith law under Wisconsin Statute §628.46 and §895.80. The state recognizes both first-party and third-party bad faith claims. Under Wisconsin law, bad faith includes:

  • Absence of reasonable basis for denying benefits — Carrier denies without reasonable investigation or supporting evidence
  • Knowledge or reckless disregard of lack of basis — Carrier knows or should know the denial is improper
  • Failure to investigate promptly and thoroughly — Not conducting reasonable investigation within reasonable timeframes
  • Refusing to pay without reasonable investigation — Denying before reviewing evidence or conducting proper investigation
  • Misrepresenting policy provisions — Telling you coverage doesn't exist when it does, or misquoting policy language
  • Not attempting good faith settlement — Lowball offers with no justification, refusing to negotiate reasonably
  • Compelling litigation through unreasonable conduct — Forcing you to sue to get what's clearly owed under the policy

Wisconsin courts apply a two-part test for bad faith: (1) absence of a reasonable basis for denying benefits, and (2) knowledge or reckless disregard of the lack of a reasonable basis. The landmark case Anderson v. Continental Ins. Co. established this framework. Wisconsin also recognizes the tort of bad faith under §895.80, which allows recovery of damages beyond the policy limits.

Step-by-Step: How to File an OCI Complaint

Step 1: Gather Your Documentation

Before filing, compile a complete documentation package. OCI complaints with strong evidence produce significantly better outcomes.

  • Policy declarations page and all relevant policy sections
  • Claim number and exact date of loss
  • All correspondence with the carrier (emails, letters, adjuster notes, recorded call summaries)
  • Carrier's estimate and any denial, reservation of rights, or coverage position letters
  • Independent contractor estimates with detailed line-item breakdowns (at least 2)
  • Photos and videos of damage (organized chronologically with timestamps)
  • Detailed timeline of key events (loss date, claim filed, adjuster visit, estimate received, demands sent, responses received)
  • Any demand letters, appeals, or formal requests you've sent
  • Documentation of financial impact (repair delays, temporary housing costs, business losses)
  • Any expert opinions or professional assessments you've obtained

Step 2: File Your Complaint Online or by Phone

Online (recommended): Visit oci.wi.gov/Pages/Consumers/FileComplaint.aspx. Complete the online form with:

  • Your complete contact information
  • Insurance company name, policy number, and agent information
  • Claim number and date of loss
  • Detailed description of the problem (be specific: what happened, when, what you've tried, why the carrier's position is unreasonable or violates policy terms, what relief you're seeking)
  • Upload supporting documents (estimates, photos, correspondence, timeline)
  • Specify whether you believe bad faith is involved and why

By phone: Call 608-266-3585 (local) or 1-800-236-8517 (toll-free). An OCI consumer affairs specialist will help you file and may request documents by email or mail.

By mail: Download the complaint form from OCI's website, complete it thoroughly, and mail with copies (not originals) of supporting documents to the address above.

Step 3: OCI Reviews and Contacts the Carrier

After you file:

  1. OCI acknowledges receipt — Usually within 5-10 business days via email or mail, with an assigned complaint number
  2. OCI forwards complaint to carrier — Carrier has 20 business days to respond in writing with complete documentation
  3. Carrier must provide detailed written explanation — Must address each issue you raised, provide supporting documentation, and justify their position with policy citations
  4. OCI reviews carrier's response — Determines if carrier violated Wisconsin law, regulations, or policy terms

Step 4: OCI Investigation and Resolution

OCI may:

  • Require corrective action — If carrier violated law or regulations, OCI can order compliance and corrective measures
  • Facilitate settlement discussions — Regulatory pressure and compliance concerns often prompt significantly better offers
  • Close complaint if no violation found — OCI provides written explanation of findings and your appeal rights
  • Refer to enforcement division — Serious or repeated violations may result in fines, sanctions, or market conduct examinations
  • Provide mediation services — OCI may offer mediation to resolve disputes in appropriate cases

You receive copies of all correspondence between OCI and the carrier. Most complaints resolve within 30-60 days, though complex cases may take 60-90 days.

Escalation Timeline: When to Take Each Step

Escalation Pathway

Days 1-30: Negotiate directly with adjuster. Submit formal demand with comprehensive documentation. Allow 15-30 days for response.

Days 30-45: If no movement or inadequate response, escalate to claims supervisor or manager. Request written explanation of valuation methodology or denial basis with supporting documentation and policy citations.

Days 45-60: If still no reasonable resolution, file OCI complaint. Wisconsin's established regulatory framework and bad faith laws often change carrier's position significantly.

Days 60-90: OCI investigates. Carrier must respond within 20 business days. Many claims settle during this period with substantial improvements to initial offers.

Days 90+: If OCI complaint doesn't resolve, consider appraisal (for valuation disputes) or consult attorney (for coverage or bad faith issues). Wisconsin's favorable bad faith framework makes attorney consultation particularly valuable for significant claims.

What OCI Can and Cannot Do

OCI can:

  • Investigate unfair claim practices and policy violations
  • Require carriers to respond in writing with complete documentation
  • Order corrective action for violations of Wisconsin law
  • Impose fines and sanctions for violations
  • Conduct market conduct examinations of carriers with repeated violations
  • Create significant regulatory pressure that prompts better settlement offers
  • Refer matters for enforcement action
  • Provide mediation services in appropriate cases

OCI cannot:

  • Order a specific settlement amount or force payment
  • Act as your lawyer, adjuster, or legal representative
  • Award damages, attorney fees, or punitive damages
  • Compel the carrier to accept your valuation
  • Provide legal advice about your rights

For payment disputes over valuation, consider invoking your policy's appraisal clause. For coverage denials or bad faith conduct, consult a Wisconsin insurance attorney. The state's established bad faith framework under §628.46 and §895.80 makes legal consultation particularly valuable for significant claims.

After Filing: What to Expect

Once you file an OCI complaint:

  • Carrier attention increases significantly — Complaints are tracked and affect carrier ratings and market conduct scores. Wisconsin regulators actively monitor carrier compliance.
  • Written responses required — Carrier must explain their position in writing with supporting documentation and policy citations, which often reveals weaknesses in their case.
  • Settlement offers typically improve — Regulatory pressure and compliance concerns frequently prompt substantially better offers within 2-4 weeks of filing.
  • Timeline clarity and accountability — OCI provides structure, deadlines, and oversight, preventing indefinite delays.
  • Potential for broader investigation — If your complaint reveals patterns of misconduct, OCI may expand investigation to other policyholders or conduct market conduct examination.

Keep copies of all OCI correspondence. If the carrier increases their offer, evaluate it carefully against your documented damages before accepting. You can always negotiate further or pursue appraisal/litigation if the offer remains inadequate.

Wisconsin-Specific Claim Requirements

Wisconsin law imposes specific requirements on carriers under Wis. Stat. §628.46 and Wis. Admin. Code Ins 6.11:

  • 5 working days to acknowledge claim — Carrier must acknowledge receipt and provide necessary forms within 5 working days of notification
  • Prompt investigation requirement — Must commence reasonable investigation promptly after receiving proof of loss
  • 15 days to request additional information — If carrier needs more documentation, must request within 15 days with specific details of what's needed and why
  • 30 days to pay after agreement — Once amount is agreed or determined, payment due within 30 days
  • Written explanation of denial — If denying, carrier must provide written explanation with specific policy provisions cited and reasonable basis for denial
  • Reasonable basis requirement — All claim decisions must have a reasonable basis supported by investigation and evidence

If the carrier misses these deadlines without reasonable cause or acts without reasonable basis, that's grounds for an OCI complaint and may support a bad faith claim under §628.46.

Wisconsin Bad Faith Damages

Wisconsin's bad faith framework under §628.46 and §895.80 provides strong consumer protection. If OCI complaint doesn't resolve your dispute and you pursue litigation, Wisconsin law allows:

  • Contract damages — Full amount owed under the policy
  • Consequential damages — Financial losses caused by the delay or denial (additional living expenses, business losses, etc.)
  • Statutory damages under §628.46 — Court may award damages for bad faith claim practices
  • Tort damages under §895.80 — Damages beyond policy limits for bad faith tort
  • Attorney fees — Prevailing policyholders can recover reasonable attorney fees in bad faith cases
  • Interest on delayed payments — Statutory interest on amounts unreasonably withheld

This established legal framework gives Wisconsin policyholders significant leverage in negotiations and makes OCI complaints particularly effective.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Filing too early — Try direct negotiation and supervisor escalation first. OCI is most effective when you've exhausted reasonable negotiation efforts.
  • Incomplete documentation — Weak complaints produce weak results. Build a comprehensive evidence package before filing.
  • Vague descriptions — Be specific: what happened, when, what you've tried, why the carrier's position is unreasonable or violates policy terms, what relief you're seeking.
  • Expecting OCI to award money — OCI investigates violations but doesn't order specific payments. Use appraisal or litigation for payment disputes.
  • Not following up promptly — Check your email and mail regularly. Respond promptly to OCI requests for information or clarification.
  • Accepting inadequate offers too quickly — Just because the carrier improves their offer doesn't mean it's fair. Compare against your documented damages.
  • Not documenting bad faith indicators — If you believe bad faith is involved, document specific examples with dates and details.
  • Not considering legal counsel — For significant claims with bad faith indicators, Wisconsin's established bad faith framework makes attorney consultation particularly valuable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I file a complaint with the Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance?

File online at oci.wi.gov/Pages/Consumers/FileComplaint.aspx or call 608-266-3585 (local) or 1-800-236-8517 (toll-free). You'll need your policy number, claim number, insurer name, and detailed description of the issue. OCI typically responds within 30-45 days.

What qualifies as bad faith in Wisconsin?

Wisconsin Statute §628.46 and §895.80 define bad faith as absence of a reasonable basis for denying benefits and knowledge or reckless disregard of that lack of basis. Bad faith includes: failure to investigate promptly, refusing to pay without reasonable investigation, misrepresenting policy terms, unreasonable delay, and compelling litigation through unreasonable conduct.

How long does OCI take to resolve complaints?

Most OCI complaints receive initial acknowledgment within 10 business days. Full investigation typically takes 30-60 days depending on complexity. The carrier must respond to OCI within 20 business days of notification.

Can OCI force my insurance company to pay my claim?

OCI cannot order a specific settlement amount, but can investigate unfair practices and require the carrier to correct violations. Regulatory pressure and compliance concerns often prompt better offers. For payment disputes, you may need appraisal or litigation.

What documentation do I need to file an OCI complaint?

Gather: policy documents, claim correspondence, adjuster estimates, contractor bids, photos of damage, timeline of events, and any demand letters sent. Include specific examples of delays, misrepresentations, or unreasonable denials with dates.

Will filing a complaint affect my insurance rates?

No. Wisconsin law prohibits carriers from raising rates or canceling policies in retaliation for filing complaints. If you experience retaliation, file an additional complaint with OCI immediately.

What happens after I file an OCI complaint?

OCI reviews your complaint, contacts the carrier, and requests a written response. The carrier must respond within 20 business days. OCI investigates and may require corrective action. You receive copies of all correspondence and the final determination.

Can I file a complaint if my claim was denied?

Yes. If the denial was improper, lacked reasonable investigation, or violated policy terms, OCI can investigate. Include documentation showing why the denial was wrong and what policy language supports coverage.

Should I hire a lawyer before filing an OCI complaint?

Not required. Most policyholders file complaints themselves. However, if the claim involves significant money, bad faith indicators, or complex coverage issues, consulting a Wisconsin insurance attorney can help, especially given the state's established bad faith framework.

What is the deadline to file an OCI complaint?

No specific deadline, but file as soon as possible. Delays weaken your case. If you're considering litigation, note that Wisconsin has a 6-year statute of limitations for breach of contract under Wis. Stat. §893.43 and separate timelines for bad faith claims.
