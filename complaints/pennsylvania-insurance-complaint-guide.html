Pennsylvania Insurance Complaint Guide: File with PID

Step-by-step guide to filing complaints with the Pennsylvania Insurance Department when your claim is denied, delayed, or underpaid.

Last reviewed: February 28, 2026

Pennsylvania Insurance Department (PID) Contact Information

Online Complaint Form: www.insurance.pa.gov/Complaints

Phone: 1-877-881-6388 (Toll-Free) or 717-787-2317 (Local)

Email: consumer@pa.gov

Mail: Pennsylvania Insurance Department, Consumer Services Bureau, 1321 Strawberry Square, Harrisburg, PA 17120

Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM Eastern Time

When to File a PID Complaint

File a complaint with the Pennsylvania Insurance Department when your insurance company engages in practices that violate Pennsylvania law or your policy terms. Common triggers include:

  • Claim denial without proper investigation — Carrier denies without inspecting damage or reviewing documentation
  • Unreasonable delay — Carrier fails to acknowledge claim within 15 working days or delays investigation without reasonable cause
  • Lowball settlement offers — Offer is 30-50% below documented repair costs with no justification
  • Refusal to negotiate in good faith — Carrier ignores demands, won't respond to documentation, or makes take-it-or-leave-it offers
  • Misrepresentation of policy terms — Carrier claims coverage doesn't exist when policy language clearly provides it
  • Bad faith tactics — Intimidation, threats to cancel policy, or coercive settlement pressure
  • Failure to provide written explanation — Carrier denies claim without explaining the basis for denial

Pennsylvania Bad Faith Law: What Qualifies

Pennsylvania has one of the strongest bad faith statutes in the nation. Under 42 Pa.C.S. §8371 and 31 Pa. Code §146.7, bad faith includes:

  • Lacking reasonable basis for denying benefits — Denial without proper investigation or reasonable justification
  • Knowing or reckless disregard of reasonable basis — Carrier knows denial is improper but denies anyway
  • Misrepresenting pertinent facts or policy provisions — Telling you coverage doesn't exist when it does
  • Failing to acknowledge communications promptly — Not responding to inquiries within 15 working days
  • Failing to investigate promptly and thoroughly — Not conducting reasonable investigation before denial
  • Refusing to pay without reasonable investigation — Denying before reviewing evidence
  • Not attempting good faith settlement — Lowball offers with no justification when liability is reasonably clear
  • Compelling litigation through unreasonable conduct — Forcing you to sue to get what's owed
  • Delaying investigation or payment — Unreasonable delays without valid cause

Pennsylvania's bad faith statute allows policyholders to recover interest, punitive damages, court costs, and attorney fees. This makes carriers particularly responsive to PID complaints, as regulatory scrutiny can lead to expensive bad faith litigation.

Step-by-Step: How to File a PID Complaint

Step 1: Gather Your Documentation

Before filing, compile a complete documentation package. PID complaints with strong evidence produce better outcomes.

  • Policy declarations page and relevant policy sections
  • Claim number and date of loss
  • All correspondence with the carrier (emails, letters, adjuster notes)
  • Carrier's estimate and any denial letters
  • Contractor estimates with line-item breakdowns
  • Photos and videos of damage
  • Timeline of key events (loss date, claim filed, adjuster visit, estimate received, demands sent, responses)
  • Any demand letters you've sent
  • Weather documentation if relevant (Pennsylvania experiences storms, winter damage, and flooding)

Step 2: File Your Complaint Online or by Phone

Online (recommended): Visit www.insurance.pa.gov/Complaints. Complete the online form with:

  • Your contact information
  • Insurance company name and policy number
  • Claim number and date of loss
  • Detailed description of the problem (be specific: what happened, when, what you've tried, why the carrier's position is wrong)
  • Upload supporting documents (estimates, photos, correspondence)

By phone: Call 1-877-881-6388 or 717-787-2317. A PID representative will help you file and may request documents by email or mail.

By mail: Download the complaint form from PID's website, complete it, and mail with copies of supporting documents to the address above.

Step 3: PID Reviews and Contacts the Carrier

After you file:

  1. PID acknowledges receipt — Usually within 2-5 business days via email or mail
  2. PID forwards complaint to carrier — Carrier has 20 business days to respond in writing
  3. Carrier must provide written explanation — Must address each issue you raised and provide documentation
  4. PID reviews carrier's response — Determines if carrier violated Pennsylvania law or policy terms

Step 4: PID Investigation and Resolution

PID may:

  • Require corrective action — If carrier violated law, PID can order compliance
  • Facilitate settlement discussions — Regulatory pressure often prompts better offers
  • Close complaint if no violation found — PID provides explanation
  • Refer to enforcement — Serious or repeated violations may result in fines or sanctions
  • Market conduct examination — Patterns of violations may trigger broader investigation

You receive copies of all correspondence. Most complaints resolve within 30-75 days.

Escalation Timeline: When to Take Each Step

Escalation Pathway

Days 1-30: Negotiate directly with adjuster. Submit formal demand with documentation. Allow 15-30 days for response.

Days 30-45: If no movement, escalate to claims supervisor. Request written explanation of valuation or denial.

Days 45-60: If still no resolution, file PID complaint. Regulatory pressure often changes carrier's position.

Days 60-90: PID investigates. Carrier must respond. Many claims settle during this period.

Days 90+: If PID complaint doesn't resolve, consider appraisal (for valuation disputes) or consult attorney (for coverage or bad faith issues). Pennsylvania's bad faith statute makes attorney consultation particularly valuable for large claims.

What PID Can and Cannot Do

PID can:

  • Investigate unfair claim practices
  • Require carriers to respond in writing
  • Order corrective action for violations
  • Impose fines and sanctions for repeated violations
  • Create regulatory pressure that prompts better offers
  • Conduct market conduct examinations for pattern violations
  • Revoke or suspend carrier licenses for serious violations

PID cannot:

  • Order a specific settlement amount
  • Force the carrier to pay your claim
  • Act as your lawyer or adjuster
  • Award damages or attorney fees
  • Provide legal advice on your specific case

For payment disputes over amount, consider invoking your policy's appraisal clause. For coverage denials or bad faith, consult a Pennsylvania insurance attorney.

After Filing: What to Expect

Once you file a PID complaint:

  • Carrier attention increases — Complaints are tracked and affect carrier ratings. Many carriers settle quickly to avoid regulatory scrutiny.
  • Written responses required — Carrier must explain their position in writing, which often reveals weaknesses in their case.
  • Settlement offers may improve — Regulatory pressure frequently prompts better offers within 2-4 weeks of filing.
  • Timeline clarity — PID provides structure and deadlines, preventing indefinite delays.
  • Bad faith exposure — Pennsylvania's strong bad faith statute makes carriers particularly responsive to PID complaints, as violations can lead to expensive litigation.

Keep copies of all PID correspondence. If the carrier increases their offer, evaluate it carefully before accepting. You can always negotiate further or pursue appraisal/litigation if needed.

Pennsylvania-Specific Claim Requirements

Pennsylvania law imposes specific requirements on carriers:

  • 15 working days to acknowledge claim — Carrier must acknowledge receipt within 15 working days of notification
  • Prompt investigation required — Must begin investigation immediately upon notification
  • 15 working days to respond to communications — Carrier must respond to policyholder inquiries within 15 working days
  • Written explanation required — Denials must include specific policy provisions, facts, or law relied upon
  • 30 days to pay after agreement — Once amount is agreed, payment must be made within 30 days
  • Interest on delayed payments — Pennsylvania law requires interest on payments delayed beyond 30 days after liability becomes clear

If the carrier misses these requirements without reasonable cause, that's grounds for a PID complaint and may support a bad faith claim under 42 Pa.C.S. §8371.

Pennsylvania Weather and Natural Disaster Considerations

Pennsylvania's diverse geography creates unique claim challenges:

  • Winter storm damage — Ice dams, roof collapse, and frozen pipes are common. Document maintenance history and show damage was caused by covered peril.
  • Wind and hail damage — Severe thunderstorms cause significant damage. Get multiple contractor estimates and document pre-existing vs. new damage.
  • Water damage and flooding — Distinguish covered water damage from excluded flood. Standard policies cover rain intrusion but exclude ground flooding.
  • Tornado damage — While less common than other states, Pennsylvania experiences tornadoes. Document wind patterns and damage severity.
  • Tree damage — Fallen trees are common. Ensure carrier properly applies coverage for tree removal and structural damage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Filing too early — Try negotiation and supervisor escalation first. PID is most effective when you've exhausted direct negotiation.
  • Incomplete documentation — Weak complaints produce weak results. Build your evidence package before filing.
  • Vague descriptions — Be specific: what happened, when, what you've tried, why the carrier is wrong, what policy language supports your position.
  • Expecting PID to award money — PID investigates violations but doesn't order specific payments. Use appraisal or litigation for payment disputes.
  • Not following up — Check your email and mail regularly. Respond promptly to PID requests for information.
  • Accepting first offer after complaint — Carriers often make improved offers after PID involvement. Evaluate carefully before accepting.
  • Missing bad faith documentation — If pursuing bad faith claim, document every delay, misrepresentation, and unreasonable action by the carrier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I file a complaint with the Pennsylvania Insurance Department?

File online at www.insurance.pa.gov/Complaints or call 1-877-881-6388 or 717-787-2317. You'll need your policy number, claim number, insurer name, and a detailed description of the issue. PID typically responds within 30-60 days.

What qualifies as bad faith in Pennsylvania?

Pennsylvania's bad faith statute 42 Pa.C.S. §8371 allows policyholders to sue for bad faith when carriers lack reasonable basis for denying benefits or fail to investigate properly. Unfair claim practices under 31 Pa. Code §146.7 include: misrepresenting facts or policy provisions, failing to acknowledge communications promptly, refusing to pay without reasonable investigation, not attempting good faith settlement, and delaying investigation or payment without reasonable cause.

How long does PID take to resolve complaints?

Most PID complaints receive initial response within 10-15 business days. Full investigation typically takes 30-75 days depending on complexity. The carrier must respond to PID within 20 business days of notification.

Can PID force my insurance company to pay my claim?

PID cannot order a specific settlement amount, but can investigate unfair practices and require the carrier to correct violations. Regulatory pressure often prompts better offers. For payment disputes, you may need appraisal or litigation.

What documentation do I need to file a PID complaint?

Gather: policy documents, claim correspondence, adjuster estimates, contractor bids, photos of damage, timeline of events, and any demand letters sent. Strong documentation increases likelihood of favorable outcome.

Will filing a complaint affect my insurance rates?

No. Pennsylvania law prohibits carriers from raising rates or canceling policies in retaliation for filing complaints. If you experience retaliation, file an additional complaint with PID.

What happens after I file a PID complaint?

PID reviews your complaint, contacts the carrier, and requests a written response. The carrier must respond within 20 business days. PID investigates and may require corrective action. You receive copies of all correspondence.

Can I file a complaint if my claim was denied?

Yes. If the denial was improper, lacked investigation, or violated policy terms, PID can investigate. Include documentation showing why the denial was wrong and what policy language supports coverage.

Should I hire a lawyer before filing a PID complaint?

Not required. Most policyholders file complaints themselves. However, if the claim involves significant money, bad faith, or complex coverage issues, consulting a Pennsylvania insurance attorney can help. Pennsylvania's bad faith statute allows for punitive damages and attorney fees.

What is the deadline to file a PID complaint?

No specific deadline, but file as soon as possible. Delays weaken your case. If you're considering litigation, note that Pennsylvania has a 4-year statute of limitations for breach of contract and 2 years for bad faith claims under 42 Pa.C.S. §8371.
