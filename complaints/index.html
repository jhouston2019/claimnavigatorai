State Insurance Complaint Guides

File complaints with your state Department of Insurance when your claim is denied, delayed, or underpaid. State-specific processes, bad faith laws, and escalation strategies.

⚠️ Filing a DOI complaint creates regulatory pressure that often prompts better settlement offers. Most complaints resolve within 30-90 days.

When to File a Department of Insurance Complaint

File a complaint with your state Department of Insurance (DOI) when your insurance company:

  • Denies your claim without proper investigation — Insufficient explanation or no documentation
  • Delays payment unreasonably — Missing state-mandated deadlines for acknowledgment or payment
  • Underpays significantly — Offer is 30-50% below documented repair costs
  • Refuses to negotiate in good faith — Won't respond to demands, ignores documentation, or makes lowball offers without justification
  • Misrepresents policy terms — Claims coverage doesn't exist when it does
  • Engages in bad faith practices — Intimidation, threats, or coercive settlement tactics

What a DOI Complaint Does

Creates Regulatory Pressure

Insurance companies must respond to DOI complaints. Carriers track complaint ratios and face regulatory scrutiny when complaints accumulate.

Forces Written Response

The carrier must provide a written explanation of their position, which often reveals weaknesses in their denial or valuation.

Triggers Investigation

The DOI reviews your documentation and the carrier's response. If the carrier violated state law, the DOI can require corrective action.

Improves Settlement Offers

Many carriers increase their offers once a complaint is filed—regulatory attention changes their calculus.

Before You File: Build Your Documentation

DOI complaints are most effective when supported by strong documentation. Before filing, gather:

  • Policy documents — Declarations page and relevant policy sections
  • Claim correspondence — All emails, letters, and adjuster notes
  • Estimates and invoices — Carrier estimate and contractor bids
  • Photos and videos — All damage documentation
  • Timeline — Key dates: loss date, claim filed, adjuster visit, estimate received, demands sent
  • Demand letters — Any formal demands you've sent to the carrier

Strong documentation increases the likelihood of a favorable outcome and speeds resolution.
