The Founder Behind Claim Command Pro

Domain expertise in insurance claim documentation—not a public adjuster, not a lawyer, but a specialist in helping policyholders prove their losses and negotiate from strength.

Positioning: What We Do (and Don't Do)

Claim Command Pro exists in a distinct space. We are not a public adjusting firm. We do not hold adjuster licenses. We are not attorneys and do not provide legal advice. What we do is document, analyze, and prepare—giving you the tools and structure to negotiate your own claim with the carrier.

This positioning matters because it shapes everything we build. Public adjusters work for a percentage of your settlement (often 10–20%). Attorneys typically take 25–40% on contingency. We charge a flat fee for access to tools, guidance, and documentation frameworks. You retain full control. You keep the settlement.

Professional Background

The founder has spent more than 15 years in property insurance and restoration. This includes work in:

  • Restoration and estimating: Building contractor estimates, scope-of-loss documents, and line-item detail for residential and commercial property damage.
  • Carrier-side workflows: Understanding how insurers evaluate claims, use Xactimate and other estimating platforms, and apply policy language.
  • Supplement strategy: Identifying gaps between carrier estimates and contractor scope, and structuring professional supplements that carriers respond to.

That background informs every feature in Claim Command Pro: our estimate comparison logic, our documentation templates, and our supplement strategy approach. We know what carriers look for—and what they often miss.

Why This Platform Was Created

After years of seeing policyholders accept low offers simply because they lacked the documentation to fight back, the founder set out to change that. The goal was never to replace public adjusters or attorneys. It was to give informed policyholders a middle path: professional-grade tools without the percentage fee.

Many policyholders can negotiate effectively if they have clear documentation, a solid estimate comparison, and a well-structured demand. Claim Command Pro exists to provide exactly that—at a fixed price that doesn't shrink your recovery.

What We Don't Claim

We do not promise specific dollar outcomes. Every claim is different. Policy terms, damage extent, carrier behavior, and local repair costs all vary. We provide tools and methodology; the results depend on your situation. We also do not guarantee that you will recover more money. We believe that proper documentation increases the odds—and our users report meaningful improvements—but we make no exaggerated guarantees.
